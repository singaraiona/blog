A new query surface landed in rayforce2 this week. It is going to surprise people.

Datalog. Rules and queries over EAV triples, evaluated semi-naively to fixpoint. The implementation underneath is substantial - `src/ops/datalog.c` is around four thousand lines - but the surface is the small part. It runs alongside the SQL-shaped query path, shares the optimiser, shares the storage. It is also, in my testing, the cleanest way to express graph queries we have ever had.

Here is what it looks like to use:

```
(set db (datoms))
(set db (assert-fact db 1 'name 'Alice))
(set db (assert-fact db 1 'dept 'Engineering))
(set db (assert-fact db 2 'name 'Bob))
(set db (assert-fact db 2 'dept 'Sales))

(query db (find ?n ?d) (where (?e :name ?n) (?e :dept ?d)))
```

A rule binds a derived predicate to a body. Recursive rules close over their own conclusion:

```
(rule (reachable ?x ?y) (?x :edge ?y))
(rule (reachable ?x ?z) (?x :edge ?y) (reachable ?y ?z))

(query gdb (find ?x ?y) (where (reachable ?x ?y)))
```

That is straight from `examples/rfl/datalog.rfl`. Negation is `(not ...)`, wildcards are `_`, attribute names are keyword-shaped (`:name`, `:dept`, `:edge`), values can be symbols (`'Alice`) or numbers.

This compiles into a DAG over the same morsel-driven pipeline as everything else. The fixpoint is a loop around the DAG. The semi-naive optimisation - re-deriving only from facts new in the previous iteration - is a single new opcode (`OP_ANTIJOIN` against the prior iteration's results). The total addition was small because the engine was already most of what Datalog needs.

This is the moment that is going to make me look smart and is in fact a coincidence of the rewrite. The morsel pipeline, the DAG optimiser, the CSR graph columns - they were all built for different reasons. Datalog walks in and finds them all useful. Semi-naive evaluation maps onto morsel-driven execution naturally because the working set of the previous iteration is exactly a morsel-shaped column. The DAG optimiser fuses rule bodies. The CSR storage makes recursive joins fast.

I did not see this coming when I planned the merge. I planned the merge to get the language and the engine into one place. The fact that the place also happens to be the right shape for a third paradigm is the kind of payoff you cannot promise in advance and cannot fake when it shows up.

I am going to write more about Datalog and rayforce2 over the next few months. There is a good chance it ends up the way most users meet the system, even if it is not the way most users came in.
