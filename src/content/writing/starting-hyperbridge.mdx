I needed an MPMC channel with async support, last week, in a Rust project I do not have permission to talk about. crossbeam-channel has the MPMC unbounded channel I wanted but no async surface; tokio's mpsc has async but is single-consumer. flume is close on paper and worked on benches I do not believe. So I spent the weekend writing my own. Today I open-sourced it.

The crate is called hyperbridge. The README is two paragraphs and the benchmark file is in the initial commit. That is on purpose: I do not want to publish a channel implementation people have to take on faith. If you do not believe my numbers you can run them.

The shape is the obvious one. A lock-free unbounded channel built around a linked list of fixed-size blocks. Each end of the channel is a `Cursor`: one `AtomicUsize` for the sequence index and one `AtomicPtr<Block<T>>` for the current block. Each block holds a fixed array of `Slot<T>`, with a `next` pointer the writer publishes when the block fills. MPSC and MPMC are the same data structure with different consumer paths. Async support comes through a small wrapper that exposes futures Sink and Stream. There are no third-party dependencies in the core; the futures support is behind a feature flag.

What I am explicitly trying to avoid:

- Hidden allocations in the hot path. Blocks are pooled and reused; allocation happens at block boundaries, not at every send.
- Lock contention on the slow path. Spinning is fine; locking is not. The channel never takes a mutex.
- Unsafe blocks without comments. There are unsafe blocks. They are commented one for one.

Inspired by crossbeam's unbounded channel; not a reimplementation of it. The thing I want that crossbeam does not give me is the futures surface, and the path of least resistance for that turned out to be a separate crate rather than a fork.

Today's commit log is short. README. Benches. Initial implementation. 0.1.0 is on crates.io. Let us see who finds it.
